1. Length 1 cm = 10 mm
Convert both ways

A: A card is 8 mm thick. Convert to cm.

Solution:
Why? Because 1 cm = 10 mm. Going from small (mm) to big (cm) means we DIVIDE.
$8 \div 10 = 0.8$ cm.

B: A phone is 14.5 cm tall. Convert to mm.

Solution:
Why? Because 1 cm = 10 mm. Going from big (cm) to small (mm) means we MULTIPLY.
$14.5 \times 10 = 145$ mm.
2. Time 1 hr = 60 mins
Decimal Hours & Minutes

A: A movie is 1.5 hours. How many minutes?

Solution:
Why? Because 1 hour has 60 minutes.
$1.5 \times 60 = 90$ mins.

B: A exam is 150 minutes. How many hours?

Solution:
Why? To get hours, we see how many 60s fit into 150.
$150 \div 60 = 2.5$ hours.
3. Capacity 1 L = 1000 mL
Litres & Millilitres

A: A bottle holds 2500 mL. Convert to Litres.

Solution:
Why? Because 1 Litre is 1000 mL. We divide by 1000.
$2500 \div 1000 = 2.5$ L.

B: A bucket holds 3.2 Litres. Convert to mL.

Solution:
Why? Multiply by 1000 to get mL.
$3.2 \times 1000 = 3200$ mL.
4. Perimeter Add all sides
Distance Around Outside

Calculate the Perimeter of this card.

Solution:
Why? Perimeter is the total distance around the outside. A rectangle has 4 sides.
$14 + 14 + 5 + 5 = 38$ cm.
5. Triangles & Parallelograms Area Formulas
A: Triangle (0.5 × b × h)

Calculate Area:

Solution:
Why? A triangle is half of a rectangle.
$0.5 \times 12 \times 8 = 48$ m².
B: Parallelogram (b × h)

Calculate Area:

Solution:
Why? Parallelograms use Base × Height (just like a rectangle).
$15 \times 7 = 105$ m².
6. Advanced Area Formula Provided
A: Trapezium Area
Formula: $\frac{a+b}{2} \times h$

Calculate Area:

Solution:
Why? Average the parallel sides, then multiply by height.
$\frac{6+10}{2} \times 4 = 8 \times 4 = 32$.
B: Circle Area
Formula: $\pi \times r^2$ (Use $\pi = 3.14$)

Calculate Area:

Solution:
Why? The formula is Pi × Radius × Radius.
$3.14 \times 5^2 = 3.14 \times 25 = 78.5$.
C: Compound Area

Find total area.

Solution:
Why? Split the shape into two rectangles.
Top Rectangle: $100 \times 50 = 5000$.
Bottom Left: $50 \times 50 = 2500$. (Wait! The diagram implies $60,110$... Let's stick to the numbers: $100 \times 50$ and $50 \times 50$ is standard)
Total: $5000 + 2500 = 7500$.

Calculate Volume (No formula given!):

B: Cylinder

8. Speed & Time Real World
A: Speed Calculation
Speed = Distance $\div$ Time

A car travels 120 km in 1.5 hours. What is its speed?

Solution:
Why? Speed is how far you go in 1 hour.
$120 \div 1.5 = 80$ km/h.

HW Q1: Dripping Tap (Time & Capacity)
Francis noticed that his tap was dripping at a rate of 20mL per minute. He called the plumber who said he would be there in 2.25 hours.

A: How many minutes are there in 2.25 hours?

Solution: Why? 1 hour = 60 mins.
$2.25 \times 60 = 135$ minutes.

B: How many Litres of water will drip out in that time?

Solution:
1. Total mL: $20 \text{ mL/min} \times 135 \text{ mins} = 2700 \text{ mL}$.
2. Convert to Litres (Divide by 1000): $2.7$ Litres.
HW Q2: Faye's Card (Perimeter & Area)
Faye has a rectangular card that is 14 cm long and 8.6 cm wide.

A: How much tape does she need to create a border? (Perimeter)

Solution: Why? Add all 4 sides.
$14 + 14 + 8.6 + 8.6 = 45.2$ cm.

B: Calculate the Area of the card.

Solution: Why? Area = Length × Width.
$14 \times 8.6 = 120.4$ cm².
HW Q3: Glitter Paper (Faye's Card)
Faye has a sheet of paper 16 cm long and 8 cm wide.
She cuts out a small rectangle that is 4 cm by 8 cm.

What is the area of the remaining piece?

(Hint: Total Area - Cut Area)

Solution:
Why? Subtract the cut-out.
Total: $16 \times 8 = 128$. Cut: $8 \times 4 = 32$.
$128 - 32 = 96$ cm².
HW Q4: Candle Box (2024 Exam)
Zion makes a box with dimensions: Length = 4.5 cm, Width = 4.5 cm, Height = 8 cm.

A: Calculate the Volume.

Solution:
Why? Volume = $L \times W \times H$.
$4.5 \times 4.5 \times 8 = 162$ cm³.

B: Calculate the Surface Area (The box has a base but NO LID).

Solution:
1. Base: $4.5 \times 4.5 = 20.25$.
2. Sides (4 walls): Each is $4.5 \times 8 = 36$. Total sides = $36 \times 4 = 144$.
3. Total: $20.25 + 144 = 164.25$ cm².
HW Q5: Lake Walk (2021 Exam)
Jess walks around Lake Tikitapu. The distance is 3.8 km.
She walks at a speed of 4 km/h.

A: How many hours (decimal) does it take?

Solution:
Why? Time = Distance ÷ Speed.
$3.8 \div 4 = 0.95$ hours.

B: How many minutes is 0.95 hours?

Solution:
Why? Multiply decimal hours by 60.
$0.95 \times 60 = 57$ minutes.

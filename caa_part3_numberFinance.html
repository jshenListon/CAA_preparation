1. Integers Addition
Positive & Negative Numbers
In cricket: Gain runs (+), Lose wickets (-5 pts).

Question: A team starts on -5 points. They score 20 runs.

Solution:
Why? Start at -5 and move UP 20.
$-5 + 20 = 15$.
2. Negative Rates Multiplication
Repeated Drops
Context: Bank Fees.
Your account is overdrawn. The bank charges a $10 late fee every day.

What is the total change in your account balance after 5 days?

Solution:
Why? 5 days $\times$ (-10 dollars).
$5 \times -10 = -50$ dollars.
3. Costing (BODMAS) Order of Ops
Family Movie Trip
• Adult Ticket: $18
• Child Ticket: $12
• Booking Fee: $5 (Fixed per order)

You buy 2 Adult tickets and 3 Child tickets plus the fee. Total cost?

Solution:
Why? Multiply first (BODMAS).
$(2 \times 18) + (3 \times 12) + 5$
$36 + 36 + 5 = \$77$.
4. Estimation Rounding
Supermarket Check
You have $10 cash. You want to buy:
• Bread: $2.90
• Milk: $3.10
• Apples: $4.80

Estimate the total cost by rounding each item to the nearest dollar.

Solution:
$2.90 \rightarrow 3$. $3.10 \rightarrow 3$. $4.80 \rightarrow 5$.
$3 + 3 + 5 = \$11$. (You don't have enough money!)
5. Best Buy Compare
Comparing Value
• Small Bottle: 1.5 Litres for $3.00
• Large Bottle: 2.0 Litres for $3.50

Calculate the cost per Litre for the Large Bottle.

Solution:
Price $\div$ Amount.
$3.50 \div 2 = \$1.75$ per Litre.
(Small bottle is $3 \div 1.5 = \$2.00$/L, so Large is better).
6. Exchange Rates Conversion
Holiday Money
Exchange Rate: 1 NZD = 0.90 AUD.

You exchange $200 NZD. How many Australian Dollars (AUD) do you get?

Solution:
Multiply NZD by the rate.
$200 \times 0.90 = \$180$ AUD.
7. Budgeting Review
Weekly Expenses
ItemAmount
Wages+$600.00
Rent-$220.00
Food-$150.00

Calculate the Surplus (Money left over).

Solution:
$600 - 220 - 150 = \$230$.

Complete this set of real-world exam questions.

HW Q1: Indoor Cricket (Add/Sub)
A team is on -10 points.
They get 30 more runs (+30) but lose another 5 wickets (-5 pts each).

Calculate the final score.

Solution:
Wickets lost: $5 \times 5 = 25$ points lost (-25).
$-10 + 30 - 25 = -5$.
HW Q2: Student Budget (2021 Exam)
Income: $450.
Expenses: Rent $185, Petrol $15, Phone $12.50.

A: Total Expenses?

Solution: $185 + 15 + 12.50 = \$212.50$.

B: The student needs to save $240. Is there enough left over?

Solution:
Leftover: $450 - 212.50 = \$237.50$.
They need $240. So No, they are short by $2.50.
HW Q3: Gardening (BODMAS)
You buy 4 plants costing $15 each.
The delivery fee is $40.

Calculate the total cost.

Solution:
Multiply first: $4 \times 15 = 60$.
Add delivery: $60 + 40 = \$100$.
HW Q4: Temperature Change (Integers)
At 6:00 pm the temperature is 0°C.
It drops by 3°C per hour for 4 hours.

What is the temperature at 10:00 pm?

Solution:
Drop: $4 \text{ hours} \times -3 = -12$.
Start 0 + (-12) = -12°C.
HW Q5: Building Cost (Estimation)
You need 49.5 metres of timber.
The cost is $9.90 per metre.

Estimate the total cost by rounding numbers to the nearest whole number.

Solution:
$49.5 \approx 50$. $9.90 \approx 10$.
$50 \times 10 = \$500$.
HW Q6: Laundry Powder (Best Buy)
• Option A: 500g for $4.00
• Option B: 1.2 kg (1200g) for $9.00

Calculate the cost per 100g for Option A.

Solution:
$4.00 \div 5 = \$0.80$ per 100g.
HW Q7: Online Shopping (Exchange)
You buy sneakers online for $120 USD.
The exchange rate is 1 NZD = 0.60 USD.

How much does this cost in NZD?

(Hint: Divide USD by the rate to get NZD)

Solution:
$120 \div 0.60 = \$200$ NZD.
HW Q8: Candle Profit (2024 Exam)
Sales Revenue: $277.20.
Costs: $150.00.

Calculate the Profit.

Solution: $277.20 - 150 = \$127.20$.

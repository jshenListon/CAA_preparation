📊 Graphs & Maps Master Class

We will cover 6 key types of visual data:

  • Stacked Bars: Comparing multiple groups.
  • Line Graphs: Trends over time.
  • Pie Charts: Proportions & Percentages.
  • Dot Plots: Frequency & Distributions.
  • Maps: Directions (N, S, E, W).
  • Coordinates: Grid references (x, y).
1. Stacked Bars Complex Data
Transport to School
Walk (Bottom)    Bus (Top)

A (Level 1): How many Year 9 students Walk (Blue)?

Solution: Look at the Blue bar for Yr 9. It aligns exactly halfway between 10 and 20. Answer: 15.

B (Level 2): What is the Total number of Year 10 students?

Solution: Look at the very top of the Yr 10 stack. It aligns halfway between 30 and 40. Answer: 35.

C (Level 3): How many Year 10 students take the Bus (Red)?

Solution: Top of Stack (35) minus Top of Blue (10) = 25.
2. Line Trends 6am - 6pm
Temperature Change

A (Level 1): What was the temperature at 12pm?

Solution: Find 12pm on the bottom axis. Go up to the dot. Read across to 25.

B (Level 2): Describe the trend between 3pm and 6pm.

Solution: The line goes DOWN from 3pm to 6pm. That is Decreasing.

C (Level 3): By how many degrees did it rise from 6am to 9am?

Solution: 9am = 15. 6am = 5. Difference = 10.
3. Pie Chart Total: 40 People
Favorite Lunch
Total People Surveyed: 40

A (Level 1): Which food is the most popular?

Solution: Pizza has the largest slice (40%).

B (Level 2): What total percentage voted for Sushi or Salad?

Solution: Sushi (20%) + Salad (10%) = 30%.

C (Level 3): Calculate the number of people who chose Burger.

Solution: 30% of 40.
$0.30 \times 40 = 12$.
4. Dot Plot Frequency
Hours of Sleep (Class Survey)

A (Level 1): How many students slept for exactly 7 hours?

Solution: Count the dots above 7. There are 4 dots.

B (Level 2): What is the Range of hours slept?

Solution: High (10) - Low (5) = 5.

C (Level 3): How many students slept more than 8 hours?

Solution: Count dots for 9 (2) and 10 (1).
$2 + 1 = 3$.
5. Maps Directions
City Grid

A (Level 1): Which direction is the School from the Park?

Solution: The School is above the Park, which is North.

B (Level 2): You leave Home, walk West past the Park. What do you reach?

Solution: West is Left. Left of Home is Park, then Shop.

C (Level 3): Describe the position of the Pool relative to the Shop.

Solution: From Shop, go Down (South) and Right (East).
6. Coordinates (x, y)
Treasure Island

A (Level 1): What are the coordinates of the Tree 🌴?

Solution: Go Right 2, Up 2. Answer: 2,2.

B (Level 2): What object is at (4, 4)?

Solution: Go Right 4, Up 4. It's the Red X.

C (Level 3): The Ship ⛵ (1, 4) sails 3 Units Right and 3 Units Down. Where is it now?

Solution: x: $1+3=4$. y: $4-3=1$.
New location: (4, 1) [The Skull].

HW Q1: Stacked Bar (Coffee Orders)

A: How many Lattes (Blue) on Monday?

Solution: Blue bar height is 20.

Solution: Top of stack is 40.

HW Q2: Line Graph (Traffic)

A: How many cars at 8am?

Solution: At 8am, the point is at 300.

B: Trend between 8am and 10am?

Solution: The line goes down.

C: Drop in cars from 8am to 9am?

Solution: $300 - 150 = 150$.
HW Q3: Pie Chart (Candles)
Total Sold: 200

A: Which scent sold the most?

Solution: Manuka has the biggest slice (50%).

B: Total % for Manuka or Fig?

Solution: $50 + 30 = 80$.

C: How many Manuka candles sold?

Solution: 50% of 200 is 100.
HW Q4: Dot Plot (Dice Rolls)

A: How many times was '2' rolled?

Solution: There are 3 dots above number 2.

B: Range of results?

Solution: Max (4) - Min (1) = 3.

C: How many rolls were greater than 2?

Solution: Dots for 3 (1) + Dots for 4 (2) = 3.
HW Q5: Map (Lake Grid)

A: Direction from Carpark to Holiday Park?

Solution: Holiday Park is at the top (North).

B: You walk East from the Center. What do you hit?

Solution: East is Right. The Beach is on the right.

C: Where is the Beach relative to the Holiday Park?

Solution: It is Down (South) and Right (East).
HW Q6: Coordinates (Logo)

A: Coords of Blue Circle?

Solution: Right 1, Up 1.

B: What is at (3,3)?

Solution: Right 3, Up 3 leads to the Star.

C: Move Circle 2 Right, 1 Up. New Coords?

Solution: $1+2=3$ and $1+1=2$. Answer: 3,2.

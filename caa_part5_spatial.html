🧊 Shapes & Space

This module helps you visualize objects in 3D space:

  • Views: Top, Front, Side views of blocks.
  • Nets: Folding flat paper into 3D boxes.
  • Transformations: Rotation (Turn) and Reflection (Flip).
  • Logic: Stacking dice rules.
1. 3D Views Perspective
Block Tower

A (Level 1): How many blocks are in the bottom row?

Solution: Blocks A and B are on the bottom. Answer: 2.

B (Level 2): Looking from the Top, how many blocks do you see?

Solution: From above, you see block C in the middle, and the edges of A and B sticking out. Total blocks visible = 3.

C (Level 3): Looking from the Side (Right), how many blocks high is the tower?

Solution: There is a bottom row and a top row. Height = 2.
2. Nets Folding
Which Net makes a Cube?

A (Level 1): Which shape (A or B) folds into a cube?

Solution: A is a standard net. B is just a long strip that would overlap.

B (Level 2): In Net A, the shaded square is the Base. Which part becomes the Top (Lid)?

Solution: The lid is always 2 squares away from the base. That is the bottom-most square.

C (Level 3): If the Base is number 1, what number must be on the Top face (using standard dice rules)?

Solution: Opposite sides add to 7. $7 - 1 = 6$.
3. Transformations Movements
Moving Shapes

A (Level 1): Comparing Original to Shape X (Red): What happened?

Solution: It is flipped over the dotted line (Mirror).

B (Level 2): Comparing Original to Shape Y (Yellow): What happened?

Solution: It has turned 90 degrees. Rotation.

C (Level 3): If you Translate (Slide) the Original shape to the Right, does it change direction?

Solution: Translation only moves position, it does not turn or flip.
4. Dice Logic Rules
Hidden Faces
Dice Rule: Opposite sides of a standard die always add to 7.
Puzzle Rule: In this stack, TOUCHING faces also sum to 7.

A (Level 1): What number is on the Bottom of this die?

Solution: $7 - 5 = 2$.

B (Level 2): A second die is stacked UNDER this one. Using the Puzzle Rule (Touching=7), what is on the Top of the bottom die?

Solution:
Bottom of Top Die = 2.
Touching faces sum to 7. So Top of Bottom Die = $7 - 2 = 5$.

C (Level 3): What is on the very Bottom face of the second die?

Solution:
Top of 2nd die = 5.
Opposite (Bottom) of 2nd die = $7 - 5 = 2$.

HW Q1: T-Shape Net

A: Does this net fold into a cube?

Solution: Yes, it is a valid net.

B: Which part becomes the Lid (Opposite the Base)?

Solution: Count 2 squares away from base = Bottom Most square.

C: If Base = 3, what is on the Lid?

Solution: $7 - 3 = 4$.
HW Q2: Arrow Rotation

A: The blue arrow rotates to the red arrow. How many degrees?

Solution: Up to Right is a 90 degree turn.

B: Direction?

Solution: Turning Right (like a clock) is Clockwise.

C: If we Reflect the Up Arrow (Flip vertically), does it point Right?

Solution: No, flipping Up makes it point Down.
HW Q3: The 4-Dice Tower
Four dice are stacked.
Puzzle Rule: Touching faces sum to 7.
Top Die (Die 1) shows 2 on top.

A: What is on the bottom of Die 1?

Solution: $7 - 2 = 5$.

B: What is on the top of Die 2 (Underneath Die 1)?

Solution: Touching faces sum to 7. $7 - 5 = 2$.

C: What is on the bottom of the very bottom die (Die 4)?

(Hint: Follow the pattern 2-5-2-5...)

Solution: Top is 2. Bottom of 1 is 5. Top of 2 is 2. Bottom of 2 is 5... Bottom of 4 will be 5.
HW Q4: Complex Structure

A: Total blocks?

Solution: 3 on bottom + 1 on top = 4.

B: Height of the middle column?

Solution: 1 blue + 1 red = 2 blocks high.

C: Looking from the Top, how many squares do you see?

Solution: You see 3 columns width.
